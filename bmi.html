Frequently Asked Questions

Is BMI different for men and women?

The BMI formula itself is the same, but healthy body fat percentages differ by gender. Women naturally carry more body fat than men at the same BMI. This calculator accounts for gender when giving personalized recommendations.

Does BMI change with age?

Yes. As we age, muscle mass tends to decrease and body fat increases. Older adults may be at higher health risk even at a "normal" BMI, while slightly higher BMI (25–27) in adults over 65 may actually be protective. This calculator factors in your age for tailored advice.

Why is this calculator only for adults 18+?

BMI for children and teenagers is calculated differently — it uses age and gender-specific percentiles from growth charts, not fixed cutoffs like adult BMI. If you need a BMI calculation for someone under 18, please consult a pediatrician.

What is a healthy BMI for women?

For women, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is generally considered healthy. However, women naturally have higher body fat than men at the same BMI, so some experts suggest a slightly lower upper threshold of 24 for women.

How can I lose weight safely?

The CDC recommends losing 1–2 pounds per week as a safe, sustainable rate. This is achieved through a combination of moderate calorie reduction (about 500 calories/day deficit) and increased physical activity. Crash diets are not recommended as they often lead to muscle loss and rebound weight gain.
